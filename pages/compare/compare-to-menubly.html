Deep Dive

1 The AI chat panel changes everything

With Menubly, a price change means logging in, finding the item, editing fields, saving. Allergen update? Another form. Sold out for the night? Toggle per dish.

With Capuzzella you open your menu page, hit ?edit=true, and talk to the AI sidebar: "Raise all pasta by €1, add a nut allergy warning to the pesto, and replace the soup of the day with tomato basil." The AI edits the draft HTML; you publish. What took ten minutes of clicking takes one sentence — perfect for kitchens that change menus daily.

2 Your website, not a separate menu host

Menubly gives you a menu page on their infrastructure — often a generic mini-site URL alongside thousands of other restaurants. Your homepage might be elsewhere entirely.

Capuzzella is your restaurant website. The menu is a first-class page — same header, same fonts, same domain guests already know. Scan the QR, land on yourrestaurant.com/menu, and they're one tap away from reservations, your story, and your events. One brand, one place.

3 Every language, one prompt

Tourist districts, airport locations, and multilingual cities need menus in more than one language. Menubly expects you to maintain separate copies or work within their language limits.

Capuzzella's AI can translate your entire menu — or spin up a dedicated /menu-de.html page — in seconds. Ask for formal German, casual Spanish, or Japanese with allergen footnotes preserved. No translation agency, no duplicate data entry.

4 A menu guests actually enjoy reading

Template menus look like template menus. Limited typography, rigid sections, same layout as the pizzeria down the street.

Tell Capuzzella's AI what you want: "Clean fine-dining layout, large dish names, small descriptions, allergen icons inline, sticky category nav on mobile." You get custom HTML tuned for thumb-scrolling guests — not a one-size-fits-all widget. Beautiful, readable, and yours.

5 QR codes — same guest experience

Menubly bundles a QR download button. Capuzzella doesn't need one — your menu URL is permanent. Generate a QR once with any free tool, print it on table tents, and forget it.

When you publish an update, the QR still works. Guests always see the latest prices, allergens, and specials. Static HTML loads instantly on mobile — no app, no login, no Menubly splash screen.

6 When Menubly might still fit

If you only need a bare-minimum menu with built-in phone ordering and payments — and you don't care about a full website, AI editing, or owning your HTML — Menubly's free tier is a valid shortcut.

But if you're building a real restaurant presence — branded site, multilingual menu, daily AI updates, allergen compliance you control — Capuzzella is the modern choice. Better editing. Better design. Better integration. A menu that lives where your brand lives.

The Bottom Line

Menubly is a form-based menu host. Capuzzella is an AI-powered restaurant website where your menu is a beautiful page you update by talking — translated to any language, designed for mobile, integrated with everything else you publish.

Stop clicking through admin panels on someone else's platform. Put the menu on your site. Open the chat panel. Say what changed. Publish. That's how restaurants should work in 2026.
