Same drag-and-drop promise — a fundamentally different architecture underneath

Wix and Squarespace are polished, all-in-one website builders. Millions of sites run on their shared platforms. Capuzzella takes a different path: single-tenant by design. Every customer gets their own isolated instance — not a row in someone else's database.

Deep Dive

1 Architecture & Privacy

Wix and Squarespace are classic multi-tenant SaaS platforms. Every customer's pages, images, and account data live on shared servers and shared databases. A breach, misconfiguration, or noisy neighbor on the platform can theoretically affect everyone.

Capuzzella is single-tenant by design. When you subscribe, Stripe checkout triggers automatic provisioning of your own isolated Render instance — complete with a dedicated disk, SQLite database, and admin account. Your content never sits alongside another customer's. That's a genuinely different — and more private, more trustworthy — architecture.

2 Provisioning & Onboarding

With Wix or Squarespace you sign up and start building immediately on their shared platform. Setup is fast, but you're always a tenant inside their walled garden.

Capuzzella provisions your instance from Stripe checkout. Pay, pick a subdomain, and the system spins up a dedicated server, deploys your private Capuzzella copy, and hands you admin credentials. You get the convenience of SaaS onboarding with the isolation of dedicated infrastructure.

3 No Lock-In

On Wix and Squarespace, your site is stored in proprietary systems. Export options exist but are limited — you don't get raw HTML files you can host anywhere without friction. Leaving means rebuilding.

Capuzzella stores plain HTML on disk. Drafts and published snapshots live in your private data/ directory — real files you can download, back up, or host elsewhere. A customer can always take their files and leave. That's a sales argument, not a weakness: you stay because the product is good, not because your content is trapped.

4 Editing Experience

Wix and Squarespace excel at visual, point-and-click editing. Drag elements, resize boxes, pick colors from palettes — no technical knowledge required. The learning curve is gentle for non-developers.

Capuzzella uses AI and natural language. Describe what you want — "add a pricing table" or "make the hero section darker" — and the AI edits your HTML directly. Less pixel-pushing, more conversation. Ideal if you care about content over canvas layout.

5 Performance

Wix and Squarespace sites are dynamically rendered or heavily JavaScript-driven. Page weight and load times vary by template and the number of widgets you add. Performance is acceptable but rarely exceptional.

Capuzzella publishes static HTML. Your live site is pre-built files served directly — no runtime page assembly, no bloated page-builder scripts. Fast by default, easy to put behind any CDN.

6 When Wix or Squarespace Wins

If you need a full online store with inventory, shipping integrations, and payment processing out of the box, Wix and Squarespace have mature e-commerce built in.

If you want the fastest path to a beautiful site with zero technical decisions — and you're comfortable with multi-tenant hosting — they're excellent choices. Capuzzella is for people who value isolation, portability, and AI-native editing over an all-in-one app marketplace.

The Bottom Line

Wix and Squarespace are great if you want a shared platform that handles everything for you. Capuzzella is for people who want their own private instance — provisioned automatically, isolated from every other customer, and built on plain HTML you actually own.

Single-tenant by design isn't a marketing phrase. It's the architecture: one customer, one Render service, one disk, one database, one admin. More private. More trustworthy. Genuinely different. And because your site is plain HTML on disk, there's no lock-in — you can always take your files and go. We think that's something to be proud of.
